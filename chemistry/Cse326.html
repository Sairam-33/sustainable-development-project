Internet for Sustainable Development

The internet is both a tool for progress and a challenge for sustainability. Learn about the problems caused by the growing internet infrastructure and how innovative solutions are being developed to address them.

Problems

Energy Consumption and Carbon Footprint

Data centers and network infrastructure consume massive amounts of energy, contributing to carbon emissions and climate change.

Digital Divide

Access to the internet remains unequal, with many communities lacking the infrastructure necessary to benefit from digital technologies.

E-Waste

Obsolete electronic devices contribute to the growing e-waste crisis, posing challenges to sustainability.

Solutions

Green Data Centers

By using renewable energy and optimizing efficiency, data centers can reduce their environmental impact.

Community Networks

Decentralized and community-run networks can provide affordable internet access to underserved areas.

Device Recycling

Recycling programs can help mitigate the e-waste crisis by encouraging the reuse and proper disposal of old devices.

Case Studies

Case Study: Smart Grids

Smart grids powered by IoT help reduce energy consumption by managing electricity distribution more efficiently.

Case Study: IoT in Agriculture

Using IoT technologies in agriculture reduces waste, optimizes water use, and promotes sustainable farming.
